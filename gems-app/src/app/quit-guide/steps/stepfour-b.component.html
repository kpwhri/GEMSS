ACE Strategies

After you quit smoking, there will be time when you want to smoke. Most of these cravings will pass within a minute or two. Over time, they will go away. Until then, use the ACE strategies to manage your cravings.

AVOID: Avoid high-risk situations where you'll be tempted to smoke. This isn't forever, just for the first month when your cravings are the strongest.

COPE: When you can't avoid the people, places or emotions that make you want to smoke, it's time to cope until the craving passes. Try these coping strategies:
  • Distract yourself
    Go for a walk, call a friend, read a book or do anything that gets your mind off smoking.
  • Use positive self-talk
    Remind yourself you can do it. Think about other times you've been strong and use that strength now.
  • Try substitution
    Instead of smoking, try chewing gum, chewing on a toothpick, brushing your teeth, eating a peppermint, snacking on low-calorie foods, or keeping your hands busy doing something else.
  • Call for support
    Call a friend or family member who supports your efforts to quit.
  • Take a hot shower
    Take a hot shower or bath to relax.
  • Take it one day at a time
    Don't worry about tomorrow, next week, or the rest of your life. Just get through today.
  • Remind yourself why
    Remind yourself why you're quitting smoking. Write down your reasons in your GEMS Journal and review the list whenever you feel tempted to smoke.
  • Relax
    Take a deep breath and let the moment pass.

ESCAPE: When avoiding and coping don’t work, escape! Excuse yourself and walk away. Don’t worry about seeming rude. Exposing others to smoke when they don’t want to breathe is rude, walking away is caring about yourself and putting your needs first.
