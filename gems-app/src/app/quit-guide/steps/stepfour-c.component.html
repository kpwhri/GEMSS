Managing negative emotions

When you first quit smoking, nicotine withdrawal can make you irritable and moody. So it's important to learn how to manage your mood without reaching for a cigarette. Try these things:

SLOW DOWN
  • Take frequent breaks during the day.
  • Take a one-day vacation and do something you enjoy.
  • Allow yourself extra time to get work done.
 PLAN FOR STRESSFUL SITUATIONS
  • This about what causes you stress and how you can avoid these situations, at least in the short term.
  • Think about what you can do instead of smoking when you are stressed. Write it down in your GEMS Journal.
USE POSITIVE SELF-TALK

Your self-talk is the thoughts in your head. Some people think their self-talk reflects their emotions, but the thoughts in your head can actually cause your emotions.

When you feel down or overwhelmed, tell yourself:

  • “It’s okay to feel bad right now. I’m still learning how NOT to smoke. This is difficult and takes time, but I WILL succeed.”
  • “I am stronger than my cravings. I will succeed and I will feel better soon.”
  • “Cigarettes don’t really make me feel better. If I give in and smoke, I will actually feel worse.”
BE ASSERTIVE
  • State your needs and goals clearly and calmly. For example: “I'm quitting smoking. Please don't offer me cigarettes or smoke when I am around.” Or “I’ve recently quit smoking and I’m not my best self. Please be patient with me right now.”
EXERCISE

Physical activity is a great way to relieve stress and feel better. Try walking at least 20 minutes a day.

FOCUS ON HOBBIES AND FRIENDS

Instead of focusing on the things you can’t do or that make you unhappy, focus on things you enjoy. This is a great time to start a new hobby, spend more time with your (non-smoking) friends, learn a new skill, or plan that dream vacation you’ve always wanted to take.

AVOID DRUGS AND ALCOHOL

Drugs and alcohol actually cause stress rather than making it go away. Limit or avoid their use, especially when you first stop smoking.

TAKE CARE OF YOURSELF

A healthy body will make you less susceptible to the effects of stress.

  • Sleep at least 8 hours every night.
  • Eat a healthy diet including lots of fresh fruits and vegetables.
  • Get 30 minutes of activity at least 4 days a week.
  • Do something you enjoy every day.

These tips can help you manage the stress of quitting smoking, but if you notice an extreme change in your mood or behavior (e.g., severe anger, hostility, or other erratic behavior), you may need additional help. Contact your doctor.
