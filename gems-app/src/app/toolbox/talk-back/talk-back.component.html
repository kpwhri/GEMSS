Learn to Talk Back

Below are some common excuses people have shared with us for not quitting smoking. Tap each to see what people told themselves to eventually overcome their excuse and finally give up cigarettes.

X “You gotta die from something.”
Hector

It's not that simple. Smoking kills you quicker and the diseases you can get are pretty bad. Cancer is painful. Emphysema slowly suffocates you. If you have a stroke, you can lose your speech or become paralyzed. Now I get it … it's not about if you die, but about how you die and how you live. I finally decided to try to live healthier and live better. If I have to die of something, I'm [continued];

X “This is too hard.”
Keisha

Quitting smoking is hard at first, but so is anything worthwhile. Finding a job is hard, moving is hard, paying my bills is hard. I've done other hard things, and I can do this, too. If millions of other people have quit smoking, so can I.

X “The withdrawal symptoms are worse.”
Margaret

Nicotine withdrawal can be bad, but it goes away after a month or two and there are medicines that the make the whole process a lot easier.

Once I learned this, I knew the time was right. I haven’t had a cigarette now for 7 months!

X “There's no harm in having just one.”;
Bob

My friends convinced me at a party that it would be okay to just have one cigarette. Well, they were wrong. Three months of hard work went down the drain. One cigarette led to two, then three, then I bought a pack and had to finish it. Before I knew it, I was back where I started. Fortunately, it has been easier to quit this second time, but I won’t be tempted to given in again so easily — there is harm in just one.

X “I'm gaining weight. Maybe I should smoke instead.”
Susan

Sure, I have put a little weight on, but honestly, it’s because I haven't paid attention to what I’m eating. I know there are things I can do to not gain weight other than smoking. If it really starts to bother me, I’ll start eating healthier — more fruits and vegetables — and maybe start walking. But for now, I’d rather not smoke and not worry about the weight. Smoking is killing me; an extra 5 pounds I can live with.
