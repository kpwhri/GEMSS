Learn from Others

We asked former smokers what advice they could share and here's what they told us.

X Deciding to quit was hard for me …
Manuel

Deciding to quit was hard for me. It took many years to convince myself I'd be better off without cigarettes. The problem was that I started smoking when I was a kid. I never really knew what it was like to be a non-smoker. I thought it meant living a life of misery, constantly feeling irritable and always wanting a cigarette. That was true when I first quit, but about a month after I stopped smoking my life started to improve. Food started tasting better. I could breathe better. I felt better than I ever imagined I could. Now I wish I'd quit 30 years sooner (or never started to begin with).

My advice:Don't let your fear of the unknown stop you from quitting smoking. It is hard at first, but it's worth it. You'll see.

X Everyone says quitting smoking …
Mia or Isabella

Everyone says quitting smoking is hard, and I’m sure it is for some people. But for me, it honestly wasn’t that bad. My husband and I decided we were ready to start a family, so my doctor recommended I stop smoking. She told me to use the nicotine patch and talk to a counselor. I did. A month later I stopped smoking and within 5 months I was pregnant!

My advice:Don’t try to quit on your own. Get the help you need from your doctor or other experts. It really makes a difference.
