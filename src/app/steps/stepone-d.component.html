Why is tobacco smoke harmful?

Tobacco smoke contains more than 7,000 chemicals, many of which are toxic. For example:
  • Hydrogen cyanide (used in chemical weapons)
  • Carbon monoxide (found in car exhaust)
  • Butane (used in lighter fluid)
  • Arsenic (used in pesticides)
  • Lead (formerly found in paint)
  • Cadmium (used to make batteries)
  • Formaldehyde (used to preserve dead bodies)

Over time, breathing these chemicals will harm your body.
