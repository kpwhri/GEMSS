Why is nicotine so addictive?

After you take a puff, it only takes about 6 seconds for nicotine to reach your brain and signal the neurons to release pleasure– causing chemicals (dopamine, serotonin, endorphins). That's why smoking feels good, and why it is addictive.

It's also why you crave cigarettes and start to feel irritable when you haven't smoked in a while. It's your body's way of saying it wants more nicotine (or more accurately — it wants more chemicals to make you feel good).
