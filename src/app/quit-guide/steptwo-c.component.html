Bupropion

Bupropion (brand names include Zyban, Wellbutrin and Buproban) is one of the three FDA-approved stop-smoking medicines. It is only available with a prescription. Most people use the generic form (bupropion). Your doctor may also prescribe Wellbutrin, which is another name for bupropion. Who can use it?

Bupropion is safe for many people, but it may not be the best choice for everyone. Talk to your doctor to find out if it is right for you.

How does it work?

Bupropion blocks nicotine receptors in the brain. This reduces withdrawal symptoms and makes it easier to stop smoking.

How do I use it?

You will start taking bupropion one week before your Quit Date.

Take it the same time each day and pair it with other activities to help you remember, like with breakfast and before bed. Consider setting a reminder alarm on your phone.

Take your pill(s) with food and a full glass of water to reduce the side effects.

Follow your doctor's instructions, but most people need to stay on this medicine for 12 weeks. Be sure to reorder your refill before you run out.

What are the side effects?
