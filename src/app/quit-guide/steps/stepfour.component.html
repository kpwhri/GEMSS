Prepare for your quit date

This checklist will help you prepare before your Quit Date. Be sure to do each of the following in the week before your Quit Date to increase your chance of success.

One Week Before Your Quit Date

X Plan your reward

Think about how much money you will save by not smoking. How else could you use this money? Make a list of things you could buy to reward yourself.

You the GEMS *link to* calculator to see how much money you will save over time.

X Stop buying cigarettes

It's important not to have cigarettes hanging around on or after your Quit Date, so plan your purchases wisely! Buy them by the pack, not the carton.

X Tell someone

Now that you've decided to stop smoking and you've gotten your medication, it's time to tell someone.

We know, that sounds scary. You don't have to tell everyone, but tell one person you trust. It's easier to quit if you have support from a good friend or family member.

The support person is someone who:

  • you trust
  • will support you, even when you're cranky and not your best, or
  • has made a hard change for themselves in the past

Don't choose someone who currently smokes. It will be harder for them to support you when they are not ready to quit smoking.

X Ask for the support you need

Ask your support person to do the following:

  • Call, text or email daily to check in after your Quit Date, especially after the first couple weeks
  • Offer positive encouragement
  • Be patient if you're having a hard day or are more irritable than usual
  • Be a friend, not a nag
 X Learn the basic skills for survival

Many people think quitting smoking is a matter of willpower: if you want it badly enough, you can will it to happen. Wrong!

Quitting smoking is about skill-power, not will-power!

Learning some basic skills will make quitting smoking easier. Familiarize yourself with these skills now, so you're ready for your quit date.

One Week Before Your Quit Date

X Clean house

Cleaning house is a good thing to do 1–2 days before your Quit Date. Get rid of everything that reminds you of smoking so you can start with a clean slate the morning of your quit date.

Wash your sheets, clothes, curtains, carpets — anything that smells like smoke.

Empty all of your ashtrays and start putting them away.

Open the windows in your home and car and let the fresh air in.

Round up any remaining cigarettes and put them in one place. Be sure to check drawers, pockets, briefcases or backpacks — anywhere you may have stashed a pack or some singles for safe keeping.

One Week Before Your Quit Date

X Final preparation

Here's what you need to do the day before your Quit Date:

  • Review your checklist — have you forgotten anything?
  • Make sure you have your stop-smoking medicine. Put it in the bathroom or on the kitchen table where you will see it first thing in the morning.
  • Get rid of any cigarettes you still have. Throw them out! It's easy to slip or relapse if you have cigarettes hanging around, but it's very hard to slip if you don't.
  • Plan something fun for your Quit Date that doesn't require a lot of mental energy — go to a movie, go shopping, take a walk with a friend. Choose an activity you enjoy, but one that won't make smoking easy.
  • Go to bed early, so you can get a good night's sleep.

Now you are ready for step 5!
