What else should I know?

Below are some common questions people ask when thinking about quitting smoking.

Is quitting smoking bad for me?

No. It's the healthiest choice you can make. Smoking is the #1 preventable cause of death and illness.

Should I use a stop-smoking medication if I decide to quit?

Yes. Using an FDA-approved stop-smoking medicine can double your chances of quitting smoking and staying quit, if you use the medicine as directed. Learn more under Step 3.

Can I continue to smoke marijuana if I stop smoking?

We recommend you stop or limit your use of marijuana when you first stop smoking, because using cannabis can make it harder to quit smoking.

If you choose to use marijuana after you've stopped smoking, avoid smoking or vaping. Both can damage your lungs.

How will I know I'm ready to stop smoking?

You probably won't ever feel 100% ready to give up cigarettes. But that doesn't mean you're not ready or that you won't be successful.

The best way to know if you are ready to quit is to try and see what happens. Even if you don't succeed, you will learn something that will make it easier to quit the next time you try.

Does smoking ever reduce anxiety and depression?

No. Smoking actually makes you feel more anxiety and stress. The chemicals you inhale when you smoke turn on your body's stress system. You may also feel stressed or anxious due to nicotine withdrawal. This is why smoking temporarily makes you feel better. But, long term, you're stuck in a vicious cycle where not smoking makes you feel stressed or anxious. This will change when you break your addiction to nicotine.
