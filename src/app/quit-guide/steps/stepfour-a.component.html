Anticipating high-risk situations

High-risk situations are those which make you want to smoke. They can be certain times of day, activities, locations and even moods.

Knowing your high-risk situations can help you avoid them or have a plan to deal with them when you first quit smoking. This is where your “skill-power” will come in handy.

Here are some common high-risk situations and strategies for dealing with each:

First thing in the morning

Brush your teeth when you get out of bed. A minty-fresh mouth will kill the desire to smoke.

Drinking coffee

Switch to tea (or, if you normally drink tea, switch to coffee).

Drinking alcohol

Drink a non-alcoholic beverage.

After meals

Food picture

Brush your teeth and go for a walk.

When stressed

Try deep breathing.

Watching TV

TV picture

Watch TV in a room where you don't typically smoke.

When driving to work

Car picture

Take a new route to work. Focusing on a new route can distract you from wanting a cigarette.

Mid-morning or mid-afternoon work breaks

Chair picture

Hang out with non-smoking friends. Drink some orange juice – it will give you a shot of energy & it will kill the desire for a cigarette.

Make a note in your GEMS Journal about your personal high-risk situations and what you could do to not smoke during each of these situations.
